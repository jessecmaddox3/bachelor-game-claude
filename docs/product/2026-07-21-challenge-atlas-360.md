# Challenge Atlas: 360 Reasons to Do Something

> **TL;DR:** This atlas contains exactly 360 challenge candidates across 20 categories. Adults are the primary audience, but family, reunion, couple, and kid-visible ideas are included. The catalog deliberately mixes laughter, discovery, connection, usefulness, mastery, permission, and substantial growth. These are editorial candidates, not automatically approved product content.

## How to Read the Atlas

Each entry includes a size and primary motivational job. **S, Spark** is usually under 15 minutes. **M, Nudge** is usually under 90 minutes. **L, Stretch** involves meaningful preparation, discomfort, or several steps. **XL, Quest** is multi-day, event-scale, costly, or consequential. The recipient should always be able to resize a challenge because burden depends on the person, not only the activity.

The motivational jobs are Play, Discovery, Activation, Mastery, Exchange, Connection, Permission, and Contribution. Many challenges serve more than one. Sensitive health, relationship, workplace, public, and family ideas will need a later review before release.

## 1. Quick Absurdity and Harmless Weirdness

These are tiny interruptions designed to create laughter without making another person the unwilling target.

1. **Human Notification** [S | Play] Chirp at a participating friend until they look up, say “notification delivered,” and reveal the challenge within 30 seconds.
2. **Royal Snack Decree** [S | Play] Announce your next snack in a 20-second royal proclamation and end with a bow.
3. **Object Press Conference** [S | Play] Become a household object while two people ask three questions; answer every question in character.
4. **Mundane Nature Documentary** [S | Play] Record 45 seconds of nature-documentary narration over an ordinary task such as folding laundry.
5. **Receipt Shakespeare** [S | Play] Perform three lines from a receipt, menu, or instruction label as tragic theater.
6. **Appliance Apology** [S | Play] Deliver a sincere 30-second apology to an appliance for one way humanity has mistreated it.
7. **Tiny Parade** [S | Play] Make five desk or kitchen objects march past the camera in a 15-second parade.
8. **Championship Hydration** [S | Play] Take one sip of water, give a victory speech, and thank at least three imaginary supporters.
9. **Accidental Mascot** [S | Connection] Choose a nearby object, give it a name and motto, then introduce it at the next group check-in.
10. **Applause Audit** [S | Play] Give yourself increasingly dramatic applause after completing three tiny ordinary tasks.
11. **Sofa Olympics** [M | Play] Invent one safe sport using a cushion, write three rules, and complete one scored heat.
12. **Epic Refrigerator Quest** [S | Play] Give someone directions to the refrigerator as though sending them on a dangerous fantasy quest.
13. **Museum of One Thing** [S | Play] Create a museum label for one ordinary object, place it beside the object, and photograph the exhibit.
14. **The Spoon Campaign** [S | Play] Make a 20-second advertisement proving that a normal spoon is revolutionary.
15. **Household Foley Artist** [S | Play] Complete one routine task while supplying at least five exaggerated live sound effects.
16. **Emoji Statue** [S | Play] Pick an emoji and create a full-body or seated statue recognizable within three guesses.
17. **Plant Pep Talk** [S | Play] Give a houseplant a 30-second motivational speech about its next stage of growth.
18. **Tap-Water Sommelier** [S | Play] Taste tap water and review it using five absurdly sophisticated tasting notes.

## 2. Secret Missions and Slow-Burn Jokes

These create anticipation through a harmless hidden pattern, then end with a reveal that lets other people enjoy the bit.

1. **Phrase Smuggler** [M | Play] Use three assigned absurd phrases naturally during a participating group hangout; finish when another player verifies all three.
2. **Secret Soundtrack** [M | Play] Hum the same recognizable tune during three separate moments; reveal after someone names it or after the third appearance.
3. **Paper Crown Cameos** [L | Play] Hide the same tiny paper crown somewhere in five ordinary photos, then ask the group to find it.
4. **Acrostic Transmission** [M | Play] Send four natural messages whose first letters spell PLAY; reveal the pattern after a guess or one hour.
5. **Mascot Migration** [L | Play] Move one small object through four increasingly unlikely household locations and document its journey as a photo sequence.
6. **Secret Helper Calling Card** [M | Contribution] Complete one genuinely useful household task and leave a silly calling card at the scene.
7. **Trivial Prophecy** [M | Play] Seal one harmless prediction about a friend’s snack, sports reaction, or favorite phrase, then reveal it before day’s end.
8. **Triple Callback** [L | Connection] Reference one shared joke through a text, an image, and a spoken line during the same day without repeating the wording.
9. **Undercover Expert** [S | Play] Deliver one accurate fact while hiding one absurd analogy inside it; listeners must identify the analogy.
10. **Secret Word Census** [M | Discovery] Choose a common word, silently tally its use for 30 minutes, then reveal the final count.
11. **Coordinated Coincidence** [L | Play] Coordinate with a co-conspirator to show up with the same color, snack, or accessory; finish when a third player notices.
12. **Emoji Plotline** [L | Play] Send three emoji-only messages that combine into a recognizable movie or shared memory, then reveal after the final guess.
13. **Secret Exhibit** [M | Play] Add a funny museum caption beside an ordinary shared-household item; reveal when someone notices or after one hour.
14. **Undercover Profession** [S | Play] Adopt an invented profession such as cloud inspector and slip three related terms into a five-minute group conversation.
15. **Compliment Constellation** [L | Connection] Deliver three sincere compliments linked by one secret theme, then reveal the theme at day’s end.
16. **Hidden Color Trail** [L | Play] Make one assigned color appear naturally in five photos or messages across a day.
17. **Commentary in Disguise** [M | Play] Adapt three recognizable sports-commentary phrases to ordinary events; finish when someone catches the pattern.
18. **The Three-Clue Mystery** [M | Connection] Leave three clues leading a friend or housemate to a tiny note, snack, or drawing.

1. **Useless TED Talk** [S | Play] Give a 60-second expert talk on a useless topic with a problem, breakthrough, and call to action.

3. **Improvised Anthem** [S | Play] Sing a four-line anthem for the room, group, pet, or current snack.
4. **Hyperlocal Weather Desk** [S | Play] Deliver a 30-second forecast for conditions in one room, including a dramatic warning.
5. **Invisible Product Pitch** [S | Play] Sell an invisible product in 45 seconds while the audience tries to determine what it does.
6. **Trailer for Tomorrow** [M | Permission] Make a 30-second movie trailer for one real thing you plan to do tomorrow.
7. **Emotional Grocery List** [S | Play] Read five shopping-list items while moving through five distinct emotions.
8. **Silent-Movie Chore** [S | Play] Perform a simple task as a 30-second silent movie with exaggerated reactions.
9. **Puppet Policy Debate** [M | Play] Use two objects as puppets debating a trivial policy and force them to reach a compromise.
10. **Mundane Halftime Speech** [S | Activation] Give someone or yourself a 45-second halftime speech before an ordinary task.
11. **Three-Character Voicemail** [S | Play] Record one harmless message in three distinct fictional character voices.
12. **Snack Court** [M | Play] Put a snack or household object on trial with a prosecutor, defender, and final verdict.
13. **Freeze-Dance Entrance** [S | Play] Enter the participating room with a five-second dance, freeze completely, then continue as normal.
14. **Genre-Swap Scene** [M | Play] Reenact one mundane shared event as horror, sports, romance, or nature documentary.
15. **Tiny Awards Ceremony** [M | Connection] Present three sincere but oddly specific awards, each followed by a 15-second acceptance speech.
16. **Courtesy Robot** [M | Play] Spend five minutes completing one task as an excessively polite robot with three spoken status updates.
17. **Headwear Hat Trick** [M | Play] Wear three improvised head coverings during one hour and take an official portrait of each.
18. **Confidently Wrong Expert** [S | Play] Answer three trivial questions with confidently invented explanations, then clearly reveal the real answers.

## 4. Friendship and Social Expansion

These turn “we should hang out” and “I should meet people” into specific, low-pressure actions.

1. **Specific Invite** [S | Connection] Invite one person to a named activity at a specific time, with an easy way to decline or suggest another date.
2. **Weak-Tie Restart** [M | Connection] Message someone you liked but have not spoken with in six months, referencing one memory and asking one real question.
3. **The Second Question** [L | Mastery] In three conversations, ask one follow-up based directly on what the other person said.
4. **Three-Person Bridge** [M | Contribution] Introduce two people who have a concrete reason to enjoy or help each other, explaining the connection in one sentence.
5. **Recurring Ritual Pitch** [L | Connection] Propose a modest repeating ritual, such as first-Tuesday lunch or a monthly match watch, to at least two people.
6. **Friendship Receipt** [M | Connection] Send someone a note naming a specific way they affected your life and the moment you realized it.
7. **Tiny Favor Ask** [M | Connection] Ask a trusted person for one helpful favor taking under ten minutes, then accept their answer graciously.
8. **Voice Note Upgrade** [S | Connection] Replace one routine text with a voice message containing a story, emotion, or real question.
9. **Interest Match** [M | Discovery] Ask an acquaintance what currently excites them, find one overlap, and suggest a concrete way to continue the topic.
10. **Newcomer Seat** [M | Contribution] At one gathering, spend five intentional minutes helping the newest or least-connected person feel included.
11. **Community Trial Run** [L | Discovery] Attend one recurring class, volunteer shift, fan gathering, or hobby event and speak to one organizer.
12. **Lunch Roulette** [M | Permission] Give a friend three inexpensive or at-home lunch options, let them choose, and handle the invitation and logistics.
13. **Skill Swap** [L | Exchange] Trade one 20-minute lesson in something you know for one 20-minute lesson from another person.
14. **Memory Remake** [M | Play] Recreate one element of an old shared memory, such as a snack, song, pose, or game, and share the result.
15. **Conversation Menu** [M | Play] Bring three unusual but answerable questions to a meal or call and let the other person choose one.
16. **Neighbor Micro-Hello** [S | Connection] Introduce yourself to one nearby person you regularly see and learn their name if they volunteer it.
17. **Existing-Friend Expansion** [L | Connection] Ask a friend to invite one new person to a low-cost group activity, then help make the invitation easy.
18. **Find a Hobby Buddy** [L | Connection] Post or send one specific invitation to join an interest-based activity and follow up with anyone who opts in.

## 5. Romance and Partnership

These create playful attention, discovery, reciprocity, and inside jokes without turning a relationship into a hidden test.

1. **Vow Easter Eggs** [L | Play] Work three short fragments or ideas from your vows into conversation across one day, then reveal after the third or when noticed.
2. **One New Secret** [M | Connection] Share one harmless story, hope, or opinion your partner has never heard, then invite but do not require one in return.
3. **Relationship Museum** [L | Connection] Select five objects connected to your relationship, give each a one-sentence label, and tour the exhibit together.
4. **Then-and-Now Photo** [M | Connection] Recreate one early relationship photo as closely as practical and place the versions side by side.
5. **Two-Song Kitchen Dance** [S | Play] Each partner chooses one song; dance together until both songs end.
6. **Past, Present, Future Compliments** [S | Connection] Give one specific compliment about who your partner was, is, and is becoming.
7. **Snack Flight for Two** [M | Play] Build a three-item blind snack or drink flight, have your partner rank it, then switch roles.
8. **Three-Clue Micro-Date** [M | Connection] Give your partner three clues about a 10-minute at-home or local date, then complete it together.
9. **Partner Press Conference** [M | Discovery] Hold a five-question press conference in which your partner is the expert on a favorite subject, then swap roles.
10. **Annoyance Swap** [M | Exchange] Each person names one 15-minute task they dislike; trade and complete both by the agreed deadline.
11. **Rom-Com Commentary** [S | Play] Narrate making coffee, packing lunch, or another caring act as the climax of a romantic comedy.
12. **Secret Fan Club** [L | Connection] Leave three specific appreciation notes signed by an invented fan-club name, then reveal the club president.
13. **First-Date Remix** [L | Play] Recreate one element of an early date for under $10, then add one deliberate twist.
14. **Two-Option Adventure** [M | Permission] One partner proposes two affordable mini-adventures; the other picks within 60 seconds and both complete it.
15. **Through Your Eyes** [L | Connection] Take five photos of things your partner would notice or appreciate, then explain each choice.
16. **Gratitude Map** [M | Connection] Place five short thank-you notes where the appreciated action usually happens.
17. **Couples Mascot** [M | Play] Invent or choose a mascot for the relationship, give it a motto, and take its official portrait.
18. **Postcard From Future Us** [M | Connection] Exchange postcards dated three years from now describing one ordinary good day together.

## 6. Family, Reunions, and Multigenerational Play

These create shared artifacts and cross-generational stories while allowing different ages and abilities to contribute differently.

1. **Catchphrase Census** [M | Play] Collect one signature phrase from five relatives and perform the set without naming who says which.
2. **Generation-Swap Interview** [M | Connection] Pair people from different generations for five questions each, then have them introduce one another using a new fact.
3. **Family Geography Map** [L | Discovery] Mark where ten relatives were born, lived, or had an adventure, then invite three location stories.
4. **Old-Photo Formation** [M | Play] Recreate the pose and arrangement of one old family group photo with whoever is available now.
5. **Mystery Relative Fact** [M | Discovery] Gather five approved surprising facts, read them anonymously, and have the family match each to a person.
6. **Family Micro-Olympics** [L | Play] Run three accessible five-minute events and crown both a points champion and a best-sport champion.
7. **Recipe Commentary Booth** [M | Play] Have one relative explain a family recipe while another provides live sports commentary.
8. **Three-Generation Talent Relay** [L | Connection] Build one three-minute act containing a live or recorded contribution from three generations.
9. **Specific Superlatives** [M | Connection] Give five positive, evidence-based awards such as “most likely to remember everyone’s order.”
10. **Name-Story Chain** [M | Connection] Have five people explain how they received a first, middle, family, or nickname.
11. **Object With a Past** [M | Discovery] Three relatives each show one object and tell a two-minute story about why it matters.
12. **Human Family Timeline** [L | Connection] Arrange at least eight family events on cards, then let relatives add two disputed or missing moments.
13. **Cousin Bingo** [M | Discovery] Fill a bingo card by finding relatives matching nine harmless facts, with no person used more than twice.
14. **Family Anthem Verse** [M | Play] Collect one word from five relatives and use all five in a four-line family anthem.
15. **Kid Boss Five** [S | Play] A child chooses the family’s safe silly voice, walking style, or drawing subject for five minutes; adults fully commit.
16. **Teach the Grown-Up** [M | Mastery] A child teaches an adult one game, drawing trick, dance, or fact; the adult demonstrates what they learned.
17. **Folded Giant Drawing** [M | Play] Each person draws one section of a creature without seeing the others, then unfold and name the result.
18. **Family Sound Orchestra** [S | Play] Assign each person one sound or motion and perform a 30-second song with a clear beginning and ending.

## 7. Sports Fandom and Competition

These make watching, discussing, and practicing sports more active without assuming athletic ability or expensive access.

1. **Off-Ball Detective** [S | Discovery] Watch ten minutes of soccer while following only one player without the ball, then report three useful movements.
2. **Formation in Snacks** [S | Play] Explain one football or soccer formation using coins, snacks, or household objects, then answer one question.
3. **Weak-Side Twenty** [M | Mastery] Complete 20 controlled passes, throws, touches, or shots using your nondominant side and count clean attempts.
4. **Sealed Prediction Card** [M | Mastery] Before a game, record five specific predictions including one tactical call, then score them after the final whistle.
5. **The Rival’s Best Case** [S | Mastery] Make a sincere three-minute argument for why a rival team, coach, or player deserves respect.
6. **Halftime Analyst** [S | Play] At halftime, record a 60-second diagnosis and one adjustment, then grade its accuracy after the match.
7. **New League Passport** [M | Discovery] Watch 30 minutes of a league, women’s competition, adaptive sport, or age level you rarely follow and choose one player to track.
8. **Rulebook Appeal** [M | Mastery] Find one disputed call, locate the governing rule, and give a verdict citing the exact standard.
9. **Family Mascot Draft** [M | Play] Each person invents a team name, colors, and mascot using only items at home; hold a secret vote.
10. **Commentary Recreation** [S | Play] Recreate 30 seconds of an iconic sports call for an ordinary household event.
11. **Player Origin Story** [M | Discovery] Learn how one athlete reached the professional level, identifying one setback and one person who helped.
12. **Backyard Combine** [M | Play] Choose three safe, scalable events such as target toss, balance, and short shuttle; record everyone’s first attempts.
13. **Sports Memory Interview** [M | Connection] Ask someone to describe their most vivid sports moment and preserve the story in audio or writing.
14. **Fantasy Trade Court** [S | Play] Propose one defensible fantasy or hypothetical trade and survive three minutes of questions from another fan.
15. **One Photo, Full Match** [M | Discovery] Take one original photograph capturing how sports appear in your town, home, or family, then title it.
16. **Technique Receipt** [M | Mastery] Film five attempts at one safe skill, identify one observable change, then try five more.
17. **The Zero-Dollar Matchday** [M | Connection] Create a game-day ritual using only food, objects, and activities already available and invite someone into it.
18. **Sports Knowledge Handicap** [M | Play] Play ten trivia questions, but let the less experienced player choose the category and one handicap rule.

## 8. Games, Trivia, and Puzzles

These favor curiosity, creative argument, and close competition over memorizing one narrow body of facts.

1. **Impossible Estimation League** [M | Play] Answer five estimation questions, reveal the answers, and score by percentage distance rather than exactness.
2. **Two Truths and One Prediction** [M | Connection] Share two true past events and one plausible future prediction; identify and save the prediction for later review.
3. **Muted-Match Commentary** [M | Play] Watch two muted minutes of a sports clip and invent commentary, then compare it with what actually happened.
4. **Category Auction** [M | Play] Bid how many items you can name in a category in 30 seconds; the highest bidder must perform or concede.
5. **Confidently Wrong Round** [S | Play] Answer five trivial questions as creatively wrong as possible; award one point for the best invented explanation.
6. **Fact or Bluff, With Receipts** [M | Discovery] Present two sourced facts and one invention; after the vote, show the sources and explain the bluff.
7. **Personalized Pub Quiz** [L | Connection] Create three rounds of five questions covering group interests, shared history, and one surprise category.
8. **Map-Pin Sprint** [M | Play] Drop pins for five prompted places without searching and score by total distance from the true locations.
9. **Alphabet Draft** [M | Play] Draft one item for each assigned letter within a category, with no duplicate answers across players.
10. **Five-Clue Identity** [S | Mastery] Describe a person, place, object, or character using clues from hardest to easiest; score the solving clue.
11. **Ridiculous Bracket** [M | Play] Seed eight mundane things into a bracket, debate each matchup for 30 seconds, and crown a champion.
12. **Memory Market** [M | Play] Wager how many items from a shared event, photo, or room you can recall before attempting the list.
13. **Trivia Revenge Study Break** [M | Mastery] Choose a weak category, study for exactly ten minutes, then play a five-question rematch.
14. **Extreme Photo Zoom** [S | Play] Show five highly cropped photos of familiar objects or places and score how quickly players identify them.
15. **Household Price Is Right** [M | Discovery] Estimate the current price of five staples, reveal receipts or listings, and score closest without going over.
16. **Shared-Lore Quiz** [M | Connection] Write five questions about shared history, take each other’s quiz, and settle disputed answers with a witness.
17. **Absurd Team Draft** [M | Play] Draft one another into roles for an imaginary mission, explain every pick, and perform one role demonstration.
18. **Two-Person Trick Shot** [M | Mastery] Design and land a safe trick shot requiring both participants within 20 attempts.

## 9. Movement and Physical Skill

These emphasize play, repeatable experiments, and personally appropriate effort rather than punishment or one standard of fitness.

1. **One-Song Athlete** [S | Activation] Choose one song and keep moving for its full duration using three repeatable movements appropriate to your body.
2. **Mobility Receipt** [S | Discovery] Test one comfortable range of motion, follow a ten-minute mobility routine, then repeat the same test.
3. **Call-a-Kilometer** [M | Connection] Make a voice call to someone you miss while walking a comfortable route for at least ten minutes.
4. **Balance Bingo** [S | Mastery] Complete five safe balance variations near stable support, holding each for ten seconds per side.
5. **Playground Reunion** [M | Play] Spend 15 minutes using a safe playground, field, or backyard for movement rather than formal exercise.
6. **Sports Shadow** [S | Discovery] Copy the controlled movements of one athlete from a short training video for ten minutes.
7. **Carry With Purpose** [M | Activation] Complete one useful carrying task with safe loads and deliberate posture without adding weight for difficulty.
8. **Desk Escape Sequence** [L | Activation] Design a four-movement, three-minute routine and complete it at three points during one workday.
9. **Dance Mirror** [M | Play] Trade 30-second dance sequences with another person for five rounds, copying rather than judging.
10. **Active Errand** [M | Permission] Complete one nearby practical errand by walking, biking, or combining transit with movement when realistic.
11. **Five-Move Relay** [M | Connection] Each participant contributes one accessible movement; complete the resulting sequence together three times.
12. **Stair Landmark** [M | Discovery] Climb a personally appropriate number of stairs or gentle inclines and note one detail visible only along the route.
13. **Form Before Speed** [S | Mastery] Record three slow repetitions of a familiar exercise, identify one form cue, then complete seven more.
14. **Movement Snack Draft** [L | Play] Write six two-minute movement options on slips, randomly draw three during the day, and complete or swap each.
15. **Recovery Tasting Flight** [M | Discovery] Try three five-minute recovery options, then rank how you feel after each.
16. **Choose-Your-Own Triathlon** [L | Mastery] Complete three different ten-minute movement blocks in one day using modes that fit your environment.
17. **Route Remix** [M | Discovery] Take a familiar walk in the opposite direction or add one unexplored block, then note three new details.
18. **Three-Date Movement Pact** [XL | Exchange] Schedule and complete the same modest movement on three dates within seven days, sharing receipts with a partner.

10. **Menu Scout** [S | Activation] Before ordering, choose two options fitting how you want to feel afterward, then decide only between those.
11. **Future-Self Lunch** [M | Exchange] Prepare tomorrow’s lunch before cleaning tonight’s kitchen and include one element you will anticipate.

## 11. Health, Reset, and Mindful Use

These are opt-in personal experiments, not diagnoses, prescriptions, public scoring, or contests in deprivation.

1. **Hydration Anchor** [L | Activation] Attach one glass or bottle of water to three existing daily cues and complete all three in one day.
2. **Caffeine Sunset** [L | Discovery] Choose a reasonable caffeine cutoff, follow it for two days, and compare bedtime alertness and morning energy.
3. **The Ten-Minute Landing** [XL | Discovery] Create a screen-light ten-minute bedtime routine, complete it on three nights, and keep only the most helpful step.
4. **Energy Field Notes** [L | Discovery] At three times in one day, record energy plus preceding food, movement, sleep, and workload; identify one testable pattern.
5. **Baseline Without a Verdict** [L | Discovery] On three occasions, privately record when, why, and how much you used alcohol or cannabis plus next-morning experience.
6. **The First-Hour Detour** [M | Permission] On one occasion when you might use, wait 60 minutes while doing a preselected enjoyable activity, then choose freely.
7. **First Round Different** [M | Discovery] At one social event, make your first drink alcohol-free and notice whether the rest of the evening changes.
8. **Choose the Finish Line First** [M | Mastery] Before one occasion, privately choose a limit or stopping time and record whether planning helped.
9. **Alternating Anchor** [M | Mastery] During one drinking occasion, alternate each alcoholic drink with a nonalcoholic option you actually enjoy.
10. **Why Tonight Card** [S | Discovery] Before using once, name the primary reason, such as taste, celebration, boredom, stress, habit, or connection.
11. **Tomorrow Wins** [L | Exchange] Choose one next-morning activity worth feeling ready for, tell someone, and make tonight’s choices in service of showing up.
12. **Ritual Rebuild** [M | Discovery] Identify the best part of a usual drinking or cannabis ritual and recreate that element once without the substance.
13. **Two-Evening Cannabis Pause** [XL | Discovery] Select two realistic evenings to skip cannabis, preplan the usual window, and note sleep, mood, and boredom without grading yourself.
14. **Change One Cue** [L | Discovery] Alter one environmental cue linked to automatic use and observe what happens across three occurrences.
15. **Pleasure Per Dollar** [L | Exchange] Calculate one typical week’s cost, redirect a chosen portion toward something enjoyable, and use it as planned.
16. **No-Explanation Script** [M | Mastery] Write and use one comfortable sentence for declining or delaying without offering a defense.
17. **Reset With a Buddy** [L | Connection] Invite one trusted person into a time-limited experiment of your own design and complete the first private check-in.
18. **Unlock the Support Door** [M | Permission] If you are concerned about control or consequences, privately identify two credible support options and save one contact method.

## 12. AI and Technology

These use real tasks, skepticism, making, teaching, and curiosity rather than treating AI adoption as a virtue by itself.

1. **Automate a Pebble** [M | Activation] Use automation or AI to eliminate one task taking under ten minutes but recurring at least monthly; demonstrate it once.
2. **Prompt Three Ways** [M | Mastery] Solve the same real task with three differently structured prompts, then save the winner with an explanation.
3. **Hallucination Hunt** [M | Discovery] Ask an AI five factual questions in a domain you know, verify every answer, and document the most convincing error.
4. **The Skeptic Interview** [L | Connection] Ask someone who avoids AI for three objections, then show one use case chosen for their actual concerns.
5. **Human Final Cut** [M | Mastery] Generate an AI draft, mark every sentence requiring human judgment, and rewrite at least three weak sections.
6. **Model Blind Taste Test** [M | Play] Give two AI tools the same task, hide their names, and have another person choose before the reveal.
7. **Privacy Boundary Card** [S | Activation] Write five kinds of information you will not paste into a consumer AI tool and place the rule where you work.
8. **Sports Scout Bot** [M | Discovery] Use AI to compare two players or teams on three sourced measures, then identify one unsupported claim.
9. **Teach the Teacher** [L | Connection] Learn one AI feature, teach it to another person, and have them complete a useful task without taking back control.
10. **The Error Museum** [M | Mastery] Save three technology mistakes with a one-line prevention rule and share them with someone who could benefit.

## Editorial Next Pass

The atlas is intentionally broad. A future launch set should not simply take the first entries or the most spectacular ones. It should select 60 to 80 challenges after rating clarity, likely fit, delight, burden accuracy, cost, adaptability, story value, reuse potential, and sensitivity. The remaining candidates should stay searchable or enter themed packs only when the relevant mode and audience are enabled.
